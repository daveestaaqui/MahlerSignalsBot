How Aurora-Signals works

We combine structured market data with rule-based scoring to surface the most interesting scenarios each day.

1. Scan

We pull real-time and delayed data for US stocks and major crypto pairs on Ethereum and Solana, tracking price action, volume, volatility, and trend structure.

2. Score

A rules engine evaluates each asset on technical structure (breakouts, bases, momentum), recent news and macro context, and liquidity filters.

3. Explain

For the strongest candidates, we generate scenario copy: potential move ranges, technical & fundamental rationales, risk notes, and an illustrative stop level.

What we analyze

Aurora-Signals focuses on instruments where data quality and liquidity are robust enough for scenario work.

  • US listed equities with sufficient average daily volume
  • BTC, ETH, SOL and major pairs on Ethereum & Solana
  • Trend, volatility, and volume relative to recent history
  • Macro headlines and earnings context (where relevant)

Signals, not advice

Each scenario describes a possible move under specific conditions, with clear language about uncertainty. We do not provide personalized financial, investment, or trading advice.

Common questions

Is this investment advice?

No. Aurora-Signals produces automated scenarios based on market data. They are for informational purposes only and are not personalized financial, investment, or trading advice.

Where does the data come from?

Equities data is sourced from providers such as Polygon, and crypto data from public APIs such as CoinGecko and major exchanges. All scenarios are derived from real market data.

Can performance be guaranteed?

No. Market outcomes are uncertain. Each scenario describes a conditional possibility, not a promise. Risk management and independent judgment remain essential.
